Course Strategy
DECADE · BROADIE
Default Club
Driver is the default. Only move down if a specific penalty requires it — don't leave driver in the bag out of comfort.

Miss Smart
When laying back, pick a number that leaves a full shot in. Half swings create inconsistency — commit to a full swing distance.

Pin System — Inside Scoring Range Only
Green
Attack the flag. Miss fat side still preferred.
Yellow
Aim middle of green. Give yourself a putt.
Red
Aim away from trouble. Bogey is fine here.
70/30 Rule
Plan for your average shot, not your best. 70% of approach shots should find the green safely. Reserve the aggressive line for truly attackable pins.
Never Short-Side
A short-sided miss is the worst result on any pin — no green, no angle, no shot. Miss on the fat side every time.

Proximity Reality (Tour avg from 150 yds)
~20 feet. Amateur average is closer to 35–40 feet. Plan your two-putt. A made birdie putt from 20 ft is a bonus, not the expectation.
Reset Your Par
From trouble, bogey is your new par. Tour pros make bogey 80%+ from recovery situations. Accept it before you swing.
The Stranger Test
Imagine someone walked you up to this ball cold — no history, no emotion. What's the smartest play? That's your shot.
The Cost
Trees at 200 yards = ~1 extra stroke vs. fairway (Broadie: 3.87 expected vs. ~3.0 from fairway). Hero shots risk turning 1 into 3.
Strategy principles based on Scott Fawcett's DECADE system (decade.golf) and Mark Broadie's strokes gained research.
